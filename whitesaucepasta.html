White Sauce Pasta

About This Recipe

White sauce pasta is a creamy and comforting meal that works well for lunch or dinner. This version focuses on quick steps so you can make it on a busy day without compromising flavor.

Ingredients

  • 200 g pasta (penne or macaroni)
  • 1 tablespoon salt (for boiling pasta)
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 3 garlic cloves, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups milk
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/2 cup grated cheese
  • 1/2 cup mixed vegetables (capsicum, corn, carrot), optional
  • 1/2 teaspoon oregano and chili flakes, optional

Steps

  1. Boil 5-6 cups of water in a large pot. Add 1 tablespoon salt and 1 tablespoon oil, then add pasta and cook until al dente according to pack instructions.
  2. Reserve 1/4 cup pasta water and drain the rest. Keep the cooked pasta aside.
  3. If using vegetables, heat a little butter in a pan and saute the vegetables on high heat for 2-3 minutes. Remove and keep aside.
  4. In the same pan, melt the remaining butter on low heat. Add chopped garlic and cook for 30-40 seconds until fragrant.
  5. Add flour and whisk continuously for 1 minute on low heat to make a smooth roux. Do not let it brown.
  6. Slowly pour in milk in batches while whisking to avoid lumps. Keep whisking until the sauce becomes smooth and slightly thick.
  7. Add black pepper, salt, and grated cheese. Mix until the cheese melts and the sauce turns creamy.
  8. Add cooked pasta and sauteed vegetables to the sauce. Mix gently and add a splash of reserved pasta water if needed for a silky coating.
  9. Finish with oregano and chili flakes, then serve hot.

Preparation Time: 30 mins

Difficulty: Easy
